    Biography

    I started my professional career working for a Member of Congress maintaining a mixed network with sites in Washington, DC and California including development new computer solutions. Additonally, I handled legislative issues including telecommunications, international relations, science, and defense issues, providing me with an in-depth understanding of Federal policy and laws. While it was interesting and challenging work, my passion for computer technology and how it can improve the lives and work of the people who are using it. That's when I started looking for a new challenge.

    I went back to school at George Washington University, and while maintaining a full time job I obtained a Master of Forensic Sciences, focused in High Tech Investigations. This provided me a depth of knowledge in file systems, networking protocols, and the methods to recover and extract information from a miriad of digital sources. This education, and working for the Software Engeering Institute, and CERT-Coordination Center, lead me to Apple, where my skills and problem solving grew and were applied in new and differnt areas.

    While at Apple, I've held two different titles but have been the primarily responsible person for managing and developing solutions to ever changing technology picture. Some of the highlights of projects I've been responsible for include data collections, knowldege management, eDiscovery productions, source code review deployment, evidence room management, mobile device collections, criminal court testimony, data warehouse deployments, and translation between engineers, lawyers, and law enforcement agents. Through this work I wanted to build better than what were commercially available. The best way to do that was to learn how to write my own software.

    Right now, I'm working full time and attending to WeCan{Code}IT flex bootcamp, where with independent work and every other weekend code jams I'm learning Java, Spring MVC, ahd full stack software development.
